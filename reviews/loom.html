What is Loom?

Loom is an AI-powered video messaging platform that lets you record your screen, camera, or both in seconds. It's become essential for remote teams who want to communicate more personally than text but faster than scheduling a meeting.

In 2026, Loom has evolved beyond simple screen recording—it's now a full video messaging suite with AI-powered features that make creating professional videos almost effortless.

Key Features

  • Instant Recording: One-click screen and camera recording in your browser
  • AI-Powered Editing: Auto-trim, stitch clips, and add enhancements
  • Smart Transcriptions: Auto-generated captions in 50+ languages
  • AI Bug Reports: Turn video recordings into Jira items automatically
  • Viewer Insights: See who's watched your videos and for how long
  • Custom Backgrounds: Professional backgrounds without needing a green screen
  • Seamless Integrations: Works with Slack, Google Workspace, Jira, and more

Pricing

Loom offers a generous free tier with up to 25 personal videos. Paid plans start at $12/month for the Pro plan, which includes unlimited recordings, advanced editing, and team features. Business and Enterprise plans offer additional security and admin controls.

✅ Pros

  • Incredibly easy to use—just one click to record
  • Free tier is surprisingly generous
  • AI transcriptions are accurate and fast
  • Seamless integration with popular tools
  • Great for asynchronous team communication
  • Mobile apps for recording on the go

❌ Cons

  • Video quality can be inconsistent on slower connections
  • Some advanced features locked behind higher tiers
  • Storage limits on free plan
  • No built-in video editing beyond trimming

Use Cases

Loom shines for:

  • Sales: Personalize outreach with video messages
  • Engineering: Provide visual context for bug reports
  • Customer Support: Troubleshoot issues with walkthrough videos
  • Design: Share feedback and ideas visually
  • Training: Create quick how-to videos for onboarding

How It Compares

Compared to traditional screen recording tools like OBS or QuickTime, Loom is far easier to use but less powerful. Against competitors like Vidyard or Bonjoro, Loom's AI features give it an edge for team collaboration. It's the best choice if you want something that "just works" without a learning curve.
